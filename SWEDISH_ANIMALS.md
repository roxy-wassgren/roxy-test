# Svenska djur

## Älg (Moose)
*   Sweden has a very dense moose population, estimated between 250,000 and 350,000 individuals.
*   Moose are found throughout the country, except for the island of Gotland.
*   Bulls can weigh up to 800 kg and stand 2.1 meters tall at the shoulder.
*   They are most active at dawn and dusk.
*   Moose collisions are a significant traffic hazard in Sweden.

## Björn (Brown Bear)
*   Sweden has approximately 2,450 to 2,800 brown bears, primarily in the central and northern parts of the country.
*   They are omnivores, with up to 90% of their diet being plant material like berries and ants, but they also hunt moose calves.
*   Swedish bears hibernate for about 5 to 7 months each year.
*   Bear attacks are extremely rare in Sweden.

## Kungsörn (Golden Eagle)
*   There are an estimated 1,200 to 1,400 reproductive golden eagles in Sweden, with about 85% in northern Sweden.
*   Unlike many other golden eagle populations, over half of Sweden's golden eagles nest in trees, often preferring old-growth forests.
*   They are Sweden's second-largest bird of prey, with a wingspan of up to 2.25 meters.
*   Their diet varies by region but includes grouse, hares, and sometimes young reindeer or deer.
*   They are red-listed as Near Threatened (NT) due to threats from forestry, wind turbines, and human disturbance.

## Ren (Reindeer)
*   All of Sweden's 250,000 to 300,000 reindeer are semi-domesticated and managed by the Sami people.
*   Reindeer herding areas cover about 40% of Sweden.
*   Reindeer migrate seasonally between mountain pastures in summer and forests in winter.
*   Both male and female reindeer grow antlers. In winter, they rely on lichens for food.
*   Challenges include climate change impacting food access and collisions with vehicles.

## Järv (Wolverine)
*   Sweden's wolverine population is estimated between 697 and 770 individuals and is classified as Vulnerable (VU).
*   They are primarily scavengers but can prey on reindeer, especially when snow conditions allow.
*   Wolverines have large paws that act like snowshoes, enabling them to move easily on deep snow.
*   They are the largest land-dwelling members of the mustelid family.
*   Licensed quota hunting has recently been introduced due to conflicts with reindeer herding, alongside threats from poaching and low genetic diversity.
